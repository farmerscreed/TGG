What Is TGG?

TGG Campus Eco-Challenge is Nigeria's first university-based environmental innovation competition, bringing together students from UNIPORT, RSU, and IAUE to develop practical solutions for campus environmental challenges.

From waste management to energy efficiency, water conservation to biodiversity — we want YOUR ideas on how to make our campuses more sustainable.

How You'll Be Judged

Your submission will be evaluated based on the following criteria

Innovation & Creativity 25%

How novel and creative is your solution? Does it bring a fresh perspective to the problem?

Feasibility & Practicality 25%

Can your solution actually be implemented? Is it realistic given resources and constraints?

Environmental Impact 25%

What positive environmental outcomes will your solution create? How measurable are they?

Presentation Quality 25%

How well did you communicate your solution? Is your proposal clear and compelling?

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate?
Any currently enrolled student at UNIPORT, RSU, or IAUE.
Can I enter as a team?
Yes, teams are encouraged. Maximum team size is 5 students.
What kinds of problems should I focus on?
Any environmental issue on your campus — waste management, water usage, energy, pollution, biodiversity, etc.
What format should my submission be in?
A text write-up plus supporting images, videos, and data uploaded through this platform. We'll provide templates.
Is there a registration fee?
No. Registration and submission are completely free.
When is the deadline?
Submissions close at 11:59 PM on the final day of the submission window. Check the timeline for exact dates.
How are winners selected?
Winners are selected by a panel of judges based on innovation, feasibility, environmental impact, and presentation quality.
What if my idea needs funding to implement?
Top teams will have the opportunity to work with university administration and sponsors to secure implementation funding.
